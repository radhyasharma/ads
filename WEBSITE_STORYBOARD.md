## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Brand Positioning

**Estate by HOMEGRAM** is the premium plotted-developments vertical of HomeGram Real Estate — Gurugram's boutique advisory since 2014. We do not chase volume. We curate land in two carefully chosen corridors: **Bahadurgarh NCR** (urban growth) and **Lansdowne Hills** (lifestyle + yield).

We sit between two extremes:
- **Above** — generic plot brokers selling raw agricultural land without infrastructure.
- **Below** — large branded developers (DLF, Godrej) where you are customer #50,000.

We are the **considered choice** for buyers who want infrastructure-ready plots, clean titles, and an advisor who actually returns calls.

### 1.2 Brand Pillars

1. **Curation** — We say no to 90% of land we evaluate. Every plot we offer is one we'd buy ourselves.
2. **Infrastructure-First** — Roads, drainage, electricity, boundary walls — built before handover, not "promised later."
3. **Transparent Title** — Every plot is RERA-aligned, freehold, government-registered. No litigation, no encumbrance.
4. **Advisory, Not Brokerage** — We don't push inventory. We match the right plot to the right buyer.
5. **Tier-2 Conviction** — Where smart capital is moving. Where infrastructure is arriving, not arrived.

### 1.3 Voice & Tone

| We Are | We Are Not |
|---|---|
| Confident & considered | Loud or salesy |
| Specific (numbers, dates, distances) | Vague ("amazing returns") |
| Investor-grade (treats reader as intelligent) | Patronising or hand-holdy |
| Quietly aspirational | Aspirational-as-spectacle (no "lifestyle" stock photos) |
| Honest about timelines & risk | Hyperbolic ("guaranteed 5x") |

**Sample lines:**
- ✅ "Plot prices in Bahadurgarh have appreciated 35× since 2006. We don't promise that pace will repeat. We do believe the next chapter has further to run."
- ❌ "Bahadurgarh is the ULTIMATE NCR investment opportunity!!"

### 1.4 Brand Promise (the line we earn)

> **"Land that's ready when you are."**

Translates the entire infrastructure-first proposition into one sentence. Works for both projects:
- Bahadurgarh: ready to build a city home when you choose.
- Lansdowne: ready to build a Himalayan retreat when you choose.

### 1.5 Tagline Options (for hero / signage)

1. **"Land that's ready when you are."** *(recommended primary)*
2. "Considered land, in considered corridors."
3. "Where capital quietly compounds."
4. "Two cities. One standard."

## 2. Audience Segments

The site must speak to **four overlapping personas** without sounding like four different sites.

### 2.1 The NCR Investor (40%)
- Profile: 35–55, salaried/business owner in Delhi NCR, household income ₹30L–1.5Cr/yr.
- Already owns 1 home in Gurugram/Noida/Delhi. Wants to deploy 25L–1.5Cr in land for capital appreciation.
- Pain: Tier-1 NCR is saturated. Doesn't trust random plot brokers. Has been burned by paperwork issues before.
- Decision lens: ROI, title clarity, exit liquidity, builder credibility.
- **Primary draw:** Bahadurgarh.

### 2.2 The Hill-Home Aspirant (30%)
- Profile: 35–60, professional/founder, dual-income family in Delhi/Gurugram/Mumbai.
- Wants a 2nd home — for family weekends, future retirement, eventual Airbnb rental.
- Pain: Mussoorie/Shimla too crowded & expensive. Doesn't know reliable Lansdowne developers. Worried about overdevelopment ruining the view.
- Decision lens: Location quality, view protection, drive time, rental potential.
- **Primary draw:** Lansdowne.

### 2.3 The NRI / Out-of-Town Diaspora (15%)
- Profile: Indian-origin in US/UK/UAE/Singapore. Wants to deploy ₹50L–3Cr in Indian real estate.
- Pain: Can't visit easily. Fears fraud. Needs end-to-end management.
- Decision lens: Trustworthiness of developer, virtual site visits, escrow/registry process clarity, NRI tax & loan info.
- **Primary draw:** Both — needs an "NRI Buyer's Guide" page.

### 2.4 The Rental-Yield Investor (15%)
- Profile: 30–50, financially literate, uses Airbnb, watches Zerodha Coin / Smallcase content.
- Pain: Equity volatility. Wants real-asset cash flow.
- Decision lens: Yield calculator, real Airbnb data, occupancy rates.
- **Primary draw:** Lansdowne (with rental-management thesis).

### 4.2 BAHADURGARH PROJECT PAGE — `bahadurgarh.html`

**Page goal:** Convert NCR investor + diaspora into qualified leads. Tell the story of *why this corridor*.

#### Section 1 — Project Hero

- **Headline:** *Where Delhi's next chapter begins.*
- **Sub:** A gated plotted township, 28 km from Connaught Place. On the Delhi Metro Green Line. With infrastructure delivered before possession.
- **Visual:** Aerial drone still or video of the actual project site (or premium representational image). Overlay: project name, location, plot sizes available.
- **Project meta strip (sticky as you scroll):** RERA No. (or "Application In Progress") · Plot sizes (sq yd) · Possession date · Pricing on request.
- **CTA:** [Schedule Site Visit] + [Download Brochure]

#### Section 2 — At a Glance

- **Layout:** 4-column stat band on dark background.
- **Stats:**
  - 28 km from Connaught Place
  - On Delhi Metro Green Line (Bahadurgarh City)
  - KMP & UER-II Expressway access
  - Plot sizes: 100 / 150 / 200 / 300 sq yd
- **Sticky CTA:** "Get Pricing & Plot Plan →"

#### Section 3 — The Investment Thesis

- **Purpose:** Educate, not just sell. This page is read by sophisticated investors.
- **Headline:** *Why Bahadurgarh, why now.*
- **Layout:** 3-part argument, each as a section block with a number.
  1. **The Metro changed the math.**
     The Green Line extension to Bahadurgarh (operational since 2018) put a Tier-2 city on Delhi's transit grid. Every other Tier-2 NCR town (Sonipat, Faridabad, Ghaziabad, Noida) saw 4–10× appreciation in the decade after Metro arrival. Bahadurgarh is mid-curve.
  2. **KMP and UER-II are reshaping logistics.**
     The Kundli–Manesar–Palwal Expressway and the upcoming Urban Extension Road II make Bahadurgarh a node in NCR's industrial spine. Industrial demand → employment → residential demand. The compounding has begun.
  3. **The pricing gap is closing.**
     Bahadurgarh land trades at 60–80% below comparable Metro-connected NCR locations (Dwarka, Sec 88 Gurugram, Noida Ext). Convergence is the only direction.
- **Visual:** Side-by-side: map of NCR with Bahadurgarh highlighted + line chart "Land Price Index 2006–2024."

#### Section 4 — The Township

- **Purpose:** Show what the buyer is actually getting.
- **Headline:** *A complete township, not a plot in a field.*
- **Layout:** Visual feature grid + master plan thumbnail.
- **Features (Hugeicons):**
  - 30–40 ft internal roads, paved & lit
  - Underground drainage & sewage
  - Underground electrical (no overhead poles)
  - Boundary wall around full township
  - 24/7 manned security + CCTV
  - Central park & landscaped greens
  - Children's play area
  - Community pavilion (Phase 2)
  - Wide entrance with grand archway
- **Master plan visual:** Stylised illustration of the township layout (color-coded plot sizes, parks, entry, roads).
- **CTA in this section:** [Download Master Plan PDF →]

#### Section 5 — Plot Sizes & Configurations

- **Layout:** 4 plot-size cards in a row.
- **Each card shows:**
  - Plot size (e.g., 150 sq yd)
  - Dimensions (e.g., 30' × 45')
  - Suitable for: 3-BHK / 4-BHK / Duplex
  - "Get pricing →" link (opens enquiry modal)
- **Sub-note:** "Pricing varies by location within township. Sample floor plans available on request."

#### Section 6 — Location & Connectivity

- **Headline:** *15 minutes from Delhi. 5 minutes from the Metro.*
- **Layout:** Embedded interactive map (Leaflet/MapBox or Google Maps) with our project pin + concentric distance markers.
- **Connectivity list:**
  - Bahadurgarh City Metro Station — 5 min drive
  - Dwarka Sec 21 — 18 km
  - IGI Airport (Delhi) — 35 km
  - Connaught Place — 28 km
  - KMP Expressway — Direct entry
  - Upcoming AIIMS Jhajjar — 12 km
  - Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC) node — 15 km

#### Section 7 — Township Walk-Through (Visual)

- **Purpose:** Replace text with visuals where text fails.
- **Format options:**
  - 60-second drone video tour
  - 360° virtual tour (Matterport-style)
  - Photo gallery (12–18 images of similar past projects, current site, infrastructure work)
- **CTA:** [Book a Site Visit →]

#### Section 8 — The Numbers (Investment Calculator)

- **Purpose:** Make the financial case tangible.
- **Layout:** Interactive calculator card.
- **Inputs:**
  - Plot size (slider: 100 / 150 / 200 / 300 sq yd)
  - Holding period (slider: 3 / 5 / 7 / 10 yrs)
  - Conservative appreciation rate (slider: 8% / 12% / 15%)
- **Outputs:**
  - Estimated value at end of period

#### Section 9 — Documentation & Legal

- **Purpose:** Address the #1 buyer fear (paperwork).
- **Headline:** *Every document. Every step. Verified.*
- **Layout:** Checklist with green tick icons.
  - ✅ RERA registration / application status
  - ✅ Land use change (CLU) approved

  - Can NRIs buy here?

## 5. Visual Design Direction

### 5.1 Mood

**One word:** *Considered.*
Not flashy. Not warm-fuzzy. Not gimmicky. Premium-but-approachable, like a wealth-management firm's website met a National Geographic photo essay.

### 5.2 Reference Sites (study, don't copy)

- **DLF.in** — clean grids, strong typography, project storytelling.
- **Lodha.com** — premium feel, video-first hero.
- **Sotheby's International Realty** — global premium real estate gold standard.
- **Aman Resorts** — premium hospitality (architecture + restraint).
- **The True American Project** (just for type/layout inspiration).

### 5.3 Color System

| Token | HEX | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--ink` | #0B0B0E | Primary dark backgrounds, body text on cream |
| `--cream` | #FAF7F2 | Primary light backgrounds |
| `--bone` | #F1ECE3 | Secondary light backgrounds, dividers |
| `--gold` | #B89758 | Primary accent (CTAs, key stats) — slightly more muted than current `#C9A84C` |
| `--gold-deep` | #8B6F36 | Hover state, deep accents |
| `--forest` | #2D3F2F | Lansdowne-only accent (used sparingly) |
| `--text-mid` | #4A4944 | Body text on cream |
| `--text-soft` | #807D75 | Secondary text on cream |
| `--ink-soft` | rgba(255,255,255,0.7) | Body text on ink |

**Discipline:** No more than 2 colors visible in any one viewport, plus white/black neutral. Gold is for emphasis only — never decoration.

### 5.4 Typography

- **Display (headlines):** *Cormorant Garamond* or *Fraunces* — both serif with strong personality. Heavy italic for "estate-like" feel.
- **Body:** *Inter* or *Söhne* — geometric, neutral, highly readable.
- **Mono (for stats/labels):** *JetBrains Mono* or *IBM Plex Mono* — engineering precision feel.

**Sizes (desktop):**
- H1: clamp(48px, 6vw, 84px), weight 400 italic
- H2: clamp(36px, 4vw, 56px), weight 400 italic
- H3: clamp(24px, 2.5vw, 36px), weight 500
- Body: 17–18px, line-height 1.65
- Mono: 11–13px, letter-spacing 0.18em, uppercase

### 5.5 Imagery Standards

- **Photography style:**
  - Architectural shots — wide-angle, golden hour or blue hour preferred
  - Lifestyle shots — minimal, never staged-looking, real families if possible
  - Aerial — drone, slow pan, never over-edited
  - **Avoid:** stock photos of Western families, generic skyscrapers, thumbs-up handshakes
- **Image treatment:** Subtle warm tone, not over-saturated. Slight vignette on hero shots.
- **Aspect ratios:** 16:9 for cinematic, 4:5 for portrait sections, 1:1 for grid.

### 5.6 Iconography

- **System:** Hugeicons (stroke-rounded variant) — already specified.
- **Sizing:** 24px standard, 32px featured, 48px hero.
- **Color:** Inherit from text color, accent only when needed.
- **Discipline:** Icons supplement, never replace, text labels.

### 5.7 Motion Principles

- **Default duration:** 400–600ms for scroll-reveals, 200ms for hovers.
- **Easing:** `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.8, 0.2, 1)` (ease-out, snappy but not bouncy).
- **Allowed transforms:** translate, scale, opacity. Never animate layout properties.
- **Scroll behaviour:**
  - Sections fade up on enter (40px translate + opacity)
  - Hero images parallax 0–20% as user scrolls
  - Numbers count up from 0 when stat-band enters viewport
- **Restraint:** No automatic looping animations except hero video. No unnecessary movement.
- **Reduced motion:** Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.

### 5.8 Layout System

- **Max content width:** 1280px.
- **Side gutters:** 24px mobile, 60px tablet, 80–120px desktop.
- **Vertical rhythm:** 8px base unit. Section padding 80–140px.
- **Grid:** 12-column with 24px gutter on desktop.
- **Asymmetry:** Use deliberate asymmetric layouts on alternate sections to break monotony (e.g., 5/7 split, 4/8 split).

## 8. Technical & Performance Standards

### 8.1 Stack

- **Build approach:** Static HTML + CSS + minimal vanilla JS, OR Astro/Eleventy if we want partial hydration.
- **Hosting:** Cloudflare Pages or Vercel (free tier sufficient initially).
- **CMS:** Phase 1 — content in code. Phase 2 — Sanity / Payload / Decap CMS for blog & FAQ.
- **Forms:** Formspree / Web3Forms / custom Cloudflare Worker → routes to CRM + email.
- **Analytics:** Google Analytics 4 + Microsoft Clarity (heatmaps + session recording, free).
- **CRM:** Zoho CRM (best for India real estate; integrates with WhatsApp Business API).

### 8.2 Performance Budget (non-negotiable)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) | < 2.0s |
| INP (Interaction to Next Paint) | < 150ms |
| CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) | < 0.05 |
| Total Blocking Time | < 200ms |
| Page weight | < 1.5 MB |
| Lighthouse Performance | > 90 |
| Lighthouse Accessibility | > 95 |
| Lighthouse SEO | > 95 |

### 8.3 Accessibility Requirements

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.
- All images have meaningful `alt` text.
- All forms have `<label>` elements and ARIA descriptions.
- Color contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for body, ≥ 3:1 for large text.
- Keyboard navigation for entire site (skip-link, focus indicators).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected.
- Screen reader tested.

### 8.4 SEO Foundation

- Semantic HTML5 (`<main>`, `<article>`, `<section>`, `<nav>`).
- Open Graph + Twitter Card metadata on every page.
- Schema.org structured data:
  - Organization (HomeGram + Estate)
  - LocalBusiness (Gurugram office)
  - RealEstateAgent
  - Place (each project, with geo coordinates)
  - FAQPage (for FAQ sections)
  - BreadcrumbList
- Sitemap.xml + robots.txt
- Canonical URLs
- Mobile-first responsive
- Page-specific title + meta description (no duplicates)
- Hreflang (only if NRI content gets multi-region treatment)
